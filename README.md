# Main-Project---Marketing-Campaign-Analysis-Dashboard
The Data is about Marketing Campaign Analysis Dashboard

Overview

The Marketing Campaign Dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of : Campaign performance across platforms, Engagement trends over time, Audience segmentation by age, Relationship between budget allocation and user engagement, events by day of week, events by day highest activity, gender influence and engagement, cost per event by name, 
Total ads, campaigns, budget, users engaged and events executed.

Objectives

Measure the overall effectiveness of marketing initiatives, Track engagement and event type trend over time, Analyze budget vs engagement correlations, Compare ad-platform performance (Facebook, Instagram, Stories, Carousel, Images, Videos), Events by age group comparison, Analyze daily and weekly engagement trends, Identify peak activity periods, Understand gender-based engagement influence, Compare cost per event across campaigns and Provide actionable insights for marketing optimization.

Insights

Overall campaign metrics : Total Budget - 2.54M, Total Ads - 200, Total Campaigns - 50, Total Events - 400K and Total Users Engaged - 9,950
This indicates a large-scale, multi-platform marketing effort with significant user interaction.
Engagement rises sharply from March to May and then drops down indicating reduced campaign performance or seasonality effects.
Higher budgets tend to result in higher engagement, but some mid-budget campaigns also perform strongly-signaling efficiency opportunities.
The 25-34 age group shows the highest participation followed by the 18-24 segment.
Facebook performs better for ad types like stories and images and Instagram shows higher engagement for certain ad types likely carousel & images.
Engagement remains steady, with friday and monday showing slightly higher totals.
A high initial spike at day 21 for the events by day and then varies and falls.
Displays engagement distribution among Male,Female and Other users. Male users lead engagement (approx.55.37%), with Female users also showing strong participation.
Compares cost efficiency across multiple campaigns. Costs are changing across campaigns due to overspending.
